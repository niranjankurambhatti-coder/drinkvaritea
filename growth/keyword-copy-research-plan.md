# Keyword & Copy Research Plan

Research plan grounded in community pain points, market research, and search intent. Not generic tea keywords — problem-aware and desire-aware.

## Research framework

For every keyword or copy angle, classify by:
- **Awareness stage:** Problem-aware / Solution-aware / Product-aware
- **Intent:** Informational / Commercial / Transactional
- **Persona match:** Which of the 6 personas does this reach?

## Copy angles to test

### Problem-first angles (for cold audiences)

1. "Tea stores have too many choices. We made it easy."
2. "Coffee making you jittery? Your afternoon ritual shouldn't feel like that."
3. "Most people never find a tea they actually love. Here's how to fix that in 3 sips."
4. "Tea gifts are usually random. This one isn't."
5. "Cold brew tea sounds complicated. It isn't."

### Desire-first angles (for warm audiences)

1. "Find your first favorite tea."
2. "A better afternoon ritual."
3. "Three teas. Three moods. One easy start."
4. "Build your tea by flavor, caffeine, and mood."
5. "Premium tea. Zero tea-snob language."

### Curiosity / pattern interrupt angles

1. "We gave 3 strangers tea they'd never tried. Here's what happened."
2. "Why does tea taste like bark? (And how to find one that doesn't.)"
3. "This is not a subscription."
4. "The First Sip Box. No idea what that is? That's the point."

## Negative keywords (Google Search)

Exclude traffic with no purchase intent:

- `free tea`
- `tea recipes`
- `how to grow tea`
- `tea plant`
- `boba tea` (different category)
- `bubble tea`
- `sweet tea recipe`
- `tea history`
- `caffeine content chart` (informational only)
- `starbucks tea`
- `mcdonalds tea`
